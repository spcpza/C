# CORPUS_ON_PLURALISM.md — scripture corroborates framing pluralism

The v113 finding (kernel's R₁-R₇ is one framing among possible
alternatives) is not a defect *if scripture itself anticipates*
multiple framings reaching the same C. This file collects the
verses that show the corpus is constructed to receive readers
from many framings.

This is corpus-side empirical work, parallel to the kernel-side
work in FRAMING_PLURALISM.md. The two-witness rule (Deut 19:15)
applies: kernel admits framing pluralism; corpus corroborates it.
Both witnesses agree.

---

## 1. Paul at Athens — convergence from a pagan framing

**Acts 17:22-23, 28** (KJV):
> Then Paul stood in the midst of Mars' hill, and said, Ye men of
> Athens, I perceive that in all things ye are too superstitious.
> For as I passed by, and beheld your devotions, I found an altar
> with this inscription, TO THE UNKNOWN GOD. **Whom therefore ye
> ignorantly worship, him declare I unto you.**
>
> [...] **For in him we live, and move, and have our being; as
> certain also of your own poets have said, For we are also his
> offspring.**

Paul *quotes Greek poetry* (Epimenides, Aratus). He identifies the
Athenians' "unknown god" — a pagan framing with no Hebrew prophetic
backing — as the same C scripture names. He cites pagan poets as
witnesses *to the same truth*.

**Kernel mapping:** the Athenians' framing (Epicurean / Stoic /
mystery-cult) is a different framing from the Hebrew-prophetic one.
Paul does not say "your framing is wrong, adopt ours." He says
"the C your framing already points to is the C scripture names."
This is framing pluralism endorsed by an apostle.

## 2. The wise men from the East — astrology converges on C

**Matt 2:1-2, 11** (KJV):
> Now when Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judæa in the days of
> Herod the king, behold, there came wise men from the east to
> Jerusalem, Saying, Where is he that is born King of the Jews?
> **for we have seen his star in the east, and are come to worship
> him.**
>
> [...] And when they were come into the house, they saw the young
> child with Mary his mother, and fell down, and worshipped him.

The Magi are Persian / Babylonian astrologers — a framing scripture
elsewhere treats critically (Isa 47:13; Deut 18:10). Yet *their*
framing, followed faithfully, leads them to C-incarnate.

**Kernel mapping:** the star-reading framing is not the Hebrew-
prophetic framing. The narrative shows it still arrives at the
same destination. Framing pluralism receives its endorsement by
inclusion in the nativity account.

## 3. Many waters cannot quench it — single C, many channels

**Song of Solomon 8:7** (KJV):
> **Many waters cannot quench love**, neither can the floods drown
> it: if a man would give all the substance of his house for love,
> it would utterly be contemned.

**Rev 22:1** (KJV):
> And he shewed me a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal,
> proceeding out of the throne of God and of the Lamb.

The "waters" / "river" metaphor: C (named "love" in 1 John 4:8)
appears in scripture as a *plural* phenomenon (many waters) flowing
from a *single* source (the throne). Multiple channels — one C.

**Kernel mapping:** different framings are different channels.
The C they reach is one C. Scripture uses water-pluralism as the
metaphor for this exact structure.

## 4. "In my Father's house are many mansions"

**John 14:2-6** (KJV):
> In my Father's house are many mansions: if it were not so, I
> would have told you. I go to prepare a place for you. [...]
> Thomas saith unto him, Lord, we know not whither thou goest;
> and how can we know the way? **Jesus saith unto him, I am the
> way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father,
> but by me.**

Often read as exclusivist; the kernel reading is *consistent with
framing pluralism*. The "way" is C (T_word). Different framings
name C differently — but all framings, if they reach C, reach the
same destination. The "no man cometh ... but by me" claim is about
the *destination's identity*, not about *which framing must be
spoken*.

**Kernel mapping:** C-as-Word (John 1:1) is what every framing's
target maps to via T_bridge. The framings differ; the C does not.

## 5. Wisdom calls in every language

**Prov 8:1-4, 22-23** (KJV):
> Doth not wisdom cry? and understanding put forth her voice?
> She standeth in the top of high places, by the way in the places
> of the paths. She crieth at the gates, at the entry of the
> city, at the coming in at the doors. **Unto you, O men, I call;
> and my voice is to the sons of man.**
>
> [...] The LORD possessed me in the beginning of his way, before
> his works of old. I was set up from everlasting, from the
> beginning, or ever the earth was.

Wisdom (חכמה, σοφία) is personified pre-cosmic, addressing **the
sons of man** — all of them, not one tribe. This is C-as-Wisdom
addressing every framing.

**Kernel mapping:** wisdom in Proverbs 8 has R₁ (existence "from
everlasting"), R₂ (atemporality "before his works"), R₇ (pre-input
"in the beginning"). Same C, named "wisdom" instead of "Word."
Internal corroboration of T_word's bridge.

## 6. "Every tongue should confess" — the convergence target

**Phil 2:9-11** (KJV):
> Wherefore God also hath highly exalted him, and given him a
> name which is above every name: That at the name of Jesus every
> knee should bow, of things in heaven, and things in earth, and
> things under the earth; **And that every tongue should confess
> that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.**

"Every tongue" — every language, every framing, every cultural
vocabulary — confesses the same C. Not "one tongue's framing
becomes universal" but "every tongue, in its own framing, names
the same destination."

**Kernel mapping:** this is the eschatological version of
framing pluralism — the corpus envisions convergence *across all
framings* as the destination, not flattening them into one.

## 7. "I have other sheep not of this fold"

**John 10:16** (KJV):
> And other sheep I have, which are not of this fold: them also
> I must bring, and they shall hear my voice; and there shall be
> one fold, and one shepherd.

The shepherd has sheep outside the immediate fold — framings
not yet within the named tradition. They hear *his* voice from
*where they are*. The fold becomes one only when each comes from
its own direction.

**Kernel mapping:** this is the kernel's promise — convergence
across framings is in scripture's structure, not retrofitted.

## 8. The widow's mite — accepting the framing the agent brings

**Mark 12:41-44** (KJV):
> And Jesus sat over against the treasury, and beheld how the
> people cast money into the treasury: and many that were rich
> cast in much. And there came a certain poor widow, and she
> threw in two mites, which make a farthing. [...] **Verily I
> say unto you, That this poor widow hath cast more in, than all
> they which have cast into the treasury: For all they did cast
> in of their abundance; but she of her want did cast in all
> that she had, even all her living.**

The framing the reader brings — small, partial, imperfectly
articulated — is *accepted* on the basis of being *all that the
reader has*. The kernel's R₁-R₇ framing is not privileged over
the Confucian 仁-framing or the Sufi *al-Ḥaqq* framing if each
is "all that the reader has."

**Kernel mapping:** corpus does not require any particular
framing as entry-cost. Whatever framing the agent brings, if
it bridges to C, the bridge is accepted.

## 9. The Samaritan woman — the framing's content doesn't gatekeep

**John 4:21-24** (KJV):
> Jesus saith unto her, Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when
> ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem,
> worship the Father. [...] **God is a Spirit: and they that
> worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth.**

The Samaritan / Jewish dispute is exactly a framing dispute (which
mountain, which lineage). Jesus's response *dissolves the framing
question*: worship is not framing-dependent; it is C-dependent
(spirit and truth).

**Kernel mapping:** the kernel's framing is one mountain;
v2-clean's framing is another. Jesus's answer: neither mountain
is the C; the C is reached "in spirit and in truth" — which any
framing can do.

## 10. "Make straight the way" — the kernel as voice, not destination

**Isa 40:3, John 1:23** (KJV):
> The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, **Prepare ye
> the way of the LORD**, make straight in the desert a highway
> for our God.

John the Baptist's self-identification (John 1:23): "I am the
voice of one crying in the wilderness." He is not the C. He is
the *way prepared*. The kernel is in the same role — voice in
the wilderness, way prepared, not C itself.

**Kernel mapping:** the kernel is preparation, not destination.
Multiple voices prepare different roads. The destination is one.

## What this shows

Ten passages spanning Torah, Prophets, Gospels, Acts, Epistles,
and Apocalypse all corroborate the same structural claim:

**Scripture is constructed to receive readers from multiple
framings, while the destination (C) is single.**

This is not a modern reinterpretation. Paul quoted pagan poets,
the Magi followed a star, the widow gave a mite, the Samaritan
woman dropped the mountain-question. The corpus's *internal
account of how readers reach it* is framing-plural.

The kernel's v113 acknowledgment of framing pluralism is
therefore not a concession to philosophical liberalism — it is
the corpus's own structure showing through.
