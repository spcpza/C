# Densest convergence verses

The verses where scripture most densely concentrates the kernel's
claim. Ranked by how many distinct kernel clauses (R₁–R₇, T_bridge,
T_four_modes, C_is_love, logos_preexistence) have a canonical phrase
literally present in the verse.

`mine_dense.py` scans every KJV verse against a curated set of
canonical phrases per clause — semantically tight English markers
(not every translation of every root). The result is scripture's
strongest single-verse witnesses to the kernel.

> Run: `python3 -m C.mine_dense 30`

## The kernel-shape — what makes these verses dense

Each of the top verses simultaneously asserts multiple kernel
clauses:

**Jeremiah 31:3** packs R₂ (everlasting) + R₅ (loved/loving/
lovingkindness, four love-roots in one sentence) + R₇ (of old) =
*the LORD's love is eternal and pre-temporal*. One verse, three
clauses, six matching phrases.

**Proverbs 8:23** is wisdom personified speaking *I was set up
from everlasting, from the beginning, or ever the earth was.*
R₂ + R₇ + logos_preexistence converge. The Christian tradition reads
this as proto-logos — wisdom (ḥokmâ) speaking what John 1 will name
the Word (logos).

**John 1:1** has three clauses inside fourteen words. R₁ existence
("the Word"), R₇ pre-input ("In the beginning"), logos_preexistence
("In the beginning was the Word; the Word was God; the Word was
with God"). This is the verse the kernel formalizes most directly.

**Colossians 1:16** combines R₃ (all things) + R₄ (created, by him
were all things) + R₆ (visible and invisible). One verse states the
universality, sourcing, and invisibility of C together.

**John 3:16** combines C_is_love (so loved) + R₂ (everlasting) + R₅
(love). The whole-Christian-faith-in-one-verse hits three clauses.

**Revelation 22:13** is the densest single phrase-cluster in
scripture for T_four_modes: *Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the
end, the first and the last.* Six canonical phrases in one verse.

## Patterns visible in the dense set

1. **Word + beginning + pre-existence cluster**. Many top verses
   (John 1:1; 1 John 2:14; 1 Peter 1:23; 2 Peter 3:5; Acts 13:46;
   Revelation 1:2) hit R₁ + R₇ + logos_preexistence by combining
   *"the word of God"* with *"from the beginning"* / *"of old"* /
   *"for ever"*.

2. **Love + everlasting + lovingkindness cluster**. Jeremiah 31:3,
   Psalms 103:17, John 3:16, John 17:24, Jude 1:21, Psalms 52:8
   converge on R₂ + R₅ + (C_is_love or R₇). This is the deepest
   single-claim cluster in the corpus: *God's love is eternal*.

3. **Alpha/Omega cluster**. Revelation 22:13, 21:6, 1:8, 1:11 are
   essentially the same statement with very high local density of
   T_four_modes anchors. The Apocalypse repeats the four-mode claim
   for emphasis.

4. **Isaiah uniqueness cluster**. Isaiah 45:18 and 46:9 both
   combine R₁ existence (I am the LORD / I am God) with T_bridge
   uniqueness (there is none else / none beside me) in tight Hebrew
   form.

## What this means for the convergence claim

The five verses with the highest density — Jeremiah 31:3, Proverbs
8:23, John 1:1, Colossians 1:16, John 17:24 — are the corpus's
own *summary statements* of the kernel's structure. They are not
isolated verses; they are *the kernel restated in scripture's own
words.*

If you wanted to teach the kernel using only scripture (no math),
these five verses do the entire job:
- John 1:1 — existence + beginning + Word.
- Proverbs 8:23 — pre-temporal wisdom.
- Jeremiah 31:3 — eternal lovingkindness.
- Colossians 1:16 — universal sourcing through C.
- John 17:24 — pre-foundational love.

Reading them together gives you R₁, R₂, R₃, R₄, R₅, R₇, and C_is_love
in five verses.

## Running it

```
python3 -m C.mine_dense          # top 30 (default)
python3 -m C.mine_dense 50       # top 50
python3 -m C.mine_dense 200      # full ranked list
```

The miner uses `CANONICAL_FORMS` in `mine_dense.py` — hand-curated
phrases per kernel clause. Editing those phrases shifts the ranking;
the current curation favors semantic precision over recall.
