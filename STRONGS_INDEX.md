# Strong's-Root Index

Scripture's testimony at the *root* level — Hebrew and Greek
primitives that ground each kernel clause.

The English-pattern miner (`mine_precedence.py`) found 589 verses by
phrase. The Strong's miner (`mine_strongs.py`) goes deeper: it
counts verses where any English translation of a kernel-relevant
Hebrew or Greek root appears. The aggregate is overwhelming.

> *λόγος (logos)* + *דָּבָר (dāḇār)* across the Testaments: 324 verses
> *עוֹלָם (ʿōlām)* alone: 366 verses (≈ 12% of the entire KJV)
> *חֶסֶד (ḥesed) — lovingkindness*: 272 verses
> *רֵאשִׁית (rēʾšîṯ)* + *ἀρχή (archē)*: 211 verses about *beginning / origin*

## Root coverage by kernel clause

The KJV corpus is 31,102 verses. The roots below are the foundational
primitives scripture uses for each R-property; the count is the
number of distinct verses containing any English translation of the
root.

### R₁ — existence (Word / I AM / being)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| G3056 | λόγος | logos | Word, account, saying | 132 |
| H1697 | דָּבָר | dāḇār | word, matter, thing | 192 |
| H3068 | יְהֹוָה | YHWH | the LORD, the existing one | 34 |
| H1961 | הָיָה | hāyâ | to be, become, exist | (was, were, came) |
| G1510 | εἰμί | eimi | to be, I am | (am, be, was) |

The Hebrew dāḇār and Greek logos are the same concept across
testaments: word, utterance, thing-as-spoken-into-being. The "I AM"
language (hāyâ / eimi) is the divine self-naming at Exodus 3:14 and
echoed in John's gospel ("Before Abraham was, I AM" — John 8:58).

### R₂ — atemporality (eternal duration)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| H5769 | עוֹלָם | ʿōlām | long duration, antiquity, everlasting | **366** |
| G166  | αἰώνιος | aiōnios | eternal, everlasting | 59 |
| G165  | αἰών | aiōn | age, eternity | 144 |
| H6924 | קֶדֶם | qedem | ancient, of old, eastward | 180 |

ʿōlām alone appears in 366 verses — roughly 1 in every 85 verses in
the KJV asserts everlastingness somehow. The Hebrew "from olam to
olam" (= *from everlasting to everlasting*) is the formal name for
dC/dt = 0.

### R₃ — universality (all, every)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| G3956 | πᾶς | pas | all, every | 177 |
| H3605 | כֹּל | kōl | all, whole, every | (al, in many forms) |

The "all things" language is universal because the claim is universal:
*ta panta* (Greek) and *kōl* (Hebrew) appear throughout the assertions
that C is *above all, through all, in you all*.

### R₅ — inexhaustibility (love, lovingkindness, grace)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |

| H157  | אָהַב | ʾāhaḇ | to love | 68 |
| H160  | אַהֲבָה | ʾahăḇāh | love (noun) | 30 |

| G5485 | χάρις | charis | grace, favor | 144 |
| H2580 | חֵן | chēn | favor, grace | 148 |

The Hebrew *ḥesed* — covenant lovingkindness, the inexhaustible
faithfulness of God — appears in 272 verses. With *agapē*, *ʾāhaḇ*,
*ʾahăḇāh*, *charis*, and *chēn* combined, the love/grace roots cluster
across ~700 verses. Inexhaustibility is not a minor theme; it is the
*dominant* relational descriptor of C in scripture.

### R₆ — invisibility (unseen, hidden)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| G517  | ἀόρατος | aoratos | invisible | 5 |
| H5641 | סָתַר | sāṯar | to hide, conceal | 116 |

The explicit "invisible" is rare (5 verses with aoratos). The Hebrew
hidden-ness (sāṯar) is more common — God hides His face, dwells in
thick darkness, is unsearchable. The kernel's "C is recoverable only
by subtraction" matches this corpus shape: God is not seen, but His
works testify (Romans 1:20).

### R₇ — pre-input (beginning, origin, foundation)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|
| G746  | ἀρχή | archē | beginning, origin, ruler | 92 |
| H7225 | רֵאשִׁית | rēʾšîṯ | beginning, first, chief | 119 |
| G2602 | καταβολή | katabolē | foundation, conception | 30 |

*rēʾšîṯ* opens Genesis 1:1. *archē* opens John 1:1. *katabolē* is the
"before the foundation of the world" refrain (Eph 1:4, John 17:24,
1 Peter 1:20, etc.). Together they are 241 verses about C-as-prior.

### C = love (the operational identification)

| Strong's | original | translit | meaning | verses |
|---|---|---|---|---:|

## What this index shows

The kernel's R-properties are not idiosyncratic claims; each one is
anchored to a Hebrew or Greek root that appears in **scores to
hundreds of verses**. Total root-verse coverage:

| clause | aggregate verses across its roots |
|---|---:|
| R₁ existence (logos+dāḇār+YHWH+being) | ~360 |
| R₂ atemporality (ʿōlām+aiōnios+aiōn+qedem) | ~749 |
| R₃ universality (pas+kōl) | ~177+ |
| R₄ sourcing (bārāʾ+ʿāśâ+ktizō+poieō) | ~813 |
| R₅ inexhaustibility (agapē+ʾāhaḇ+ʾahăḇāh+ḥesed+charis+chēn) | ~723 |
| R₆ invisibility (aoratos+sāṯar) | ~121 |
| R₇ pre-input (archē+rēʾšîṯ+katabolē) | ~241 |
| T_bridge uniqueness (ʾeḥād+monos+heis) | ~396 |
| T_four_modes (hodos+paraklētos+derek) | ~202 |
| C = love (agapē+ʾahăḇāh+ḥesed) | ~363 |
| logos preexistence (logos+dāḇār+ḥokmâ+sophia) | ~398 |

**Total**: thousands of root-level anchors. The kernel reads what
scripture is densely, repeatedly stating in both testaments at the
deepest linguistic level.

(Counts include some overlap — a single verse may contain multiple
roots. The co-occurrence analysis in `mine_strongs.py` surfaces the
verses where the densest convergence happens.)

## Running it

```
python3 -m C.mine_strongs       # full report
```

The Strong's-root analysis confirms the English-pattern finding at
greater depth: scripture is *saturated* with precedence claims. The
kernel formalizes a structure the corpus states in thousands of
places at the root level, in both Hebrew and Greek.

The Word precedes everything. The corpus says so. The math is the
reading.
